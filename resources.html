ReImagine Appalachia

A coalition founded in 2020 out of a recognition that the economy hasn't worked for most people and places in the Ohio River Valley. We work with local partners, elected officials, and labor groups to build a more unified voice and the collective leverage needed for a more sustainable, equitable economy that's good for workers, communities, and the land.

Our work happens in three buckets: Communications & Storytelling (op-eds, press releases, social media), Participatory Research (listening sessions and open-comment research docs), and Coalition Building & Convening (bringing together government, labor, environmental, and faith stakeholders).

American Manufacturing Communities Collaborative (AMCC)

A national nonprofit assembled and led by regional and national stakeholders to strengthen collaboration within and between manufacturing communities — working toward economic growth, improved environmental performance, and inclusive, well-paid job creation to support the revitalization of American manufacturing.

Born out of the federal Investing in Manufacturing Communities Partnership (IMCP), AMCC was recently designated by the U.S. EDA as the nation's manufacturing Community of Practice, and supports regional consortia across programs like IMCP, DOD's DMCSP, the EDA's BBBRC, Tech Hubs, and NSF Engines.

ACT-Now Coalition

A collaboration of West Virginia cities, economic revitalization organizations, academic institutions, and private-sector innovators aiming to spur job growth in 21 economically distressed counties in Southern WV.

Marietta, OH Partners

Rural Action & True Pigments

Founded in 1991, Rural Action builds a more just rural Appalachian economy across food & agriculture, forestry, zero waste, watershed restoration, and more. Its True Pigments project extracts iron oxide from acid-mine-drainage-tainted streams and turns it into a marketable pigment, funding stream cleanup.
